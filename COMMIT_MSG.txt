Finding 1 (doc 08 §2.1 deviation clause — recorded, not worked around):
C3's workers > 1 Connect-worker set has NO working Kubernetes leg.
providerkit.ReachableURLs' per-ordinal addressing
(runtime.OrdinalName(name, i) -> EnsureReachable) resolves only literal
ordinal-named objects, which exist for StatefulSet ordinals
(StableIdentity: true — redpanda brokers, minio nodes) but never for the
Deployment shape (StableIdentity: false, docs/adr/004) that
debezium/s3sink's workers > 1 opts into. Reproduced live: a Binding on a
workers: 2 debezium Provider fails at apply with
`no member of "<name>" (2 ordinals) is currently reachable` — a hard
failure, not a drift misreport. Affects debezium and s3sink (shared code
path). Consequence for the GA decision: evidence is complete for
mid-apply-kill recoverability and DLQ-on-K8s; an unconditional
KubernetesRuntime GA claim should either exclude Connect-worker HA
(workers > 1) on Kubernetes or wait for this fix. Owner's call.
